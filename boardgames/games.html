NoGo

NoGo uses ordinary Go placement, but no move may capture stones or be suicidal.

A move is illegal if it would remove an opposing group or leave the played group without liberties.

The usual losing condition is being the first player with no legal move.

Anti-connect five / four / three

These games reverse “complete the target line to win” into “complete the target line to lose.”

Anti-five, anti-four, and anti-three share the same core rule and differ only in target line length.

Zhen Qi / Quake Gomoku

After a stone is placed, an adjacent stone in each of the eight directions is pushed one step outward. It moves into an empty target, leaves the board if pushed past an edge, and stays put if the target is occupied.

After movement resolves, either color wins by making four horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. A rule parameter selects the winner when both colors make four simultaneously. Passing loses immediately.

Xing Qi / Symmetry Game

Same-color stones form groups through eight-neighbor connectivity. A group of at least six is removed when it is symmetric across the horizontal axis, vertical axis, or either diagonal of its bounding box.

Removing a shape of n stones scores n − 5 points. The default target is four points; the first player to reduce the remaining target to zero wins. Passing loses.

King's Move Connect 4

The first move may use any empty point. Every later move must be within the eight-neighborhood of at least one opposing stone—the squares a chess king could reach in one step.

There is no gravity. The first player to make four horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins.

Nijie's AntiGomoku

On a turn, either place one of your stones on an empty point, or move an opposing stone like a chess queen along an unobstructed line; its original point is then replaced with your stone.

When either color makes five, that color loses. Passing loses; a full board with no five is a draw.
